London

London, the capital of England and the United Kingdom, is a modern city with a history dating back to Roman times. At its center stand the imposing Parliament, the iconic Big Ben and Westminster Abbey, the coronation site of British monarchs. On the other side of the Thames, the London Eye, the Ferris wheel, offers a panoramic view of the South Bank Center and the entire city.

Paris

Paris, the capital of France, is a major European city and a global center of art, fashion, gastronomy and culture. Its 19th century urban landscape is crossed by wide boulevards and the Seine. In addition to landmarks like the Eiffel Tower and the 12th-century Gothic Notre-Dame Cathedral, the city is known for its cafés and luxury boutiques lining Rue du Faubourg-Saint-Honoré.

Tokyo

Japan's bustling capital, Tokyo combines ultra-modern and traditional, illuminated skyscrapers and historic temples. The opulent Meiji Shinto shrine is renowned for its imposing gate and the woods that surround it. The imperial palace (or Kōkyo) is set in vast public gardens. The city's many museums offer exhibitions ranging from classical art (Tokyo National Museum) to a recreation of a kabuki theater (Edo-Tokyo Museum).

New York

New York is a city made up of 5 boroughs at the mouth of the Hudson River and the Atlantic Ocean. At its center is Manhattan, a densely populated borough that is among the world's major commercial, financial and cultural centers. Its must-see sites include skyscrapers like the Empire State Building and the immense Central Park. The Broadway Theater is located in Times Square.
